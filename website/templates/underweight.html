Underweight

Description

Being underweight means having a body weight below the standard threshold, with a BMI less than 18.5. Individuals in this category often have low muscle mass, small bones, and a slender appearance. Underweight can result from insufficient food intake, excessive physical activity, or certain health conditions such as thyroid toxicity or genetic factors.

Causes

  • Inadequate food intake.
  • Excessive physical activity.
  • Genetic factors.
  • Certain health conditions like thyroid toxicity or genetic predisposition.

    • Risks

  • Weakness and easy fatigue.
  • Weakened immune system.
  • Osteoporosis.
  • Irregular menstrual cycles.
  • Reduced fertility.
  • Increased risk of mortality.

    • Recommendations

  • Consume a balanced diet from all five food groups.
  • Emphasize high-protein foods like meat, milk, eggs, and legumes.
  • Engage in regular, moderate exercise.
  • Consult a healthcare professional for personalized advice.
